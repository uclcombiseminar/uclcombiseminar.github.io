<p>The last two decades have witnessed a growing trend towards proving sparse random analogues of combinatorial theorems. One unified approach to proving such theorems, formalised by Conlon and Gowers, involves establishing a 'transference principle' which allows one to translate between robust properties in the dense setting to the sparse random setting, provided the density is not too small. In this talk I will present some upcoming results which extend the previous literature by generalising a transference theorem of the aforementioned authors. We apply this result to obtain a counting lemma in the sparse regime for (hyper)graphs which are not necessarily strictly balanced, obtaining an asymptotically optimal probability of success. Joint work with Peter Allen, Julia Boettcher, and Domenico Mergoni Cecchelli.</p>
